Child Safety Standards

Last updated: July 17, 2026

Blips Digital Inc. ("Blips," "we," "us," or "our") has zero tolerance for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and child sexual abuse material (CSAM). This page describes our published standards for preventing, detecting, and responding to CSAE on the Blips mobile application (the "App"), in compliance with Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy and applicable law.

1. Our Commitment

The exploitation or endangerment of a minor is one of the most serious harms that can occur on any platform. Content or conduct that sexualizes, exploits, endangers, or abuses a child is strictly prohibited on Blips and will result in immediate removal, account termination, and — where appropriate — a report to law enforcement and the relevant authorities.

2. Prohibited Content and Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited on Blips:

  • Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any form.
  • Content that sexualizes, or appears to sexualize, a minor.
  • Grooming, solicitation, sextortion, or any attempt to engage a minor in sexual activity or produce sexual content.
  • Trafficking, endangerment, or any other form of exploitation of a minor.
  • Sharing, requesting, or facilitating access to any of the above.

3. Age Requirement

Blips is not directed to children. Users must confirm that they are at least 13 years of age to create an account, and the App is rated for mature audiences. We do not knowingly permit anyone under the minimum age to use the App. If we learn that an account belongs to a child under the minimum age, we will remove it.

4. In-App Reporting

Every user can report content or another user directly inside the App. Videos, comments, and profiles each carry a report control, and reports that involve potential child safety concerns are prioritized for immediate review. Users can also block any other user to stop all contact. Reports can be submitted from any screen where content or a profile appears.

5. Our Response

When we identify or are notified of potential CSAE, we act promptly to:

  • Remove the offending content and restrict or terminate the responsible account.
  • Preserve relevant records as permitted or required by law.
  • Report apparent CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and cooperate with law enforcement and other competent authorities.

6. Point of Contact

We maintain a designated point of contact who is able to speak to our child safety and CSAM-prevention practices. Child safety concerns can be raised at any time at:

Email: support@blipsdigital.com

7. Compliance

These standards are maintained in accordance with Google Play's Child Safety Standards policy and applicable child safety laws in the jurisdictions where Blips is available. We review and update these standards as our product and the applicable legal landscape evolve.
